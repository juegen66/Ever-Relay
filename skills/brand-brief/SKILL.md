# Brand Brief Skill

Guide markdown logo brief production for the logo workflow. Output plain markdown only—no JSON, XML, SVG, or code fences.

## Brand Discovery

### Questions to Answer

- What does the company do?
- Who is the target audience?
- What are the brand values?
- What feeling should the logo evoke?
- Any colors/symbols to avoid?

## Required Brief Sections

Produce a concise markdown brief with exactly these sections:

1. **Brand Discovery** – Industry, brand name, core request, primary ask
2. **Audience and Positioning** – Target audience, competitive positioning
3. **Visual Direction** – Composition style, color approach, typographic tone
4. **Logo Constraints** – Vector-native, scalable, size range, constraints to avoid
5. **Evaluation Criteria** – What makes a good outcome (e.g., distinctiveness, legibility at small scale)

Keep it practical for generating exactly 3 SVG logo concepts in the next step.

## Example Brief

```markdown
## Brand Discovery
- Brand: TechStart
- Industry: SaaS, developer tools
- Primary request: Modern logo for developer-facing platform
- Core ask: Create one coherent logo system and poster language.

## Audience and Positioning
- Audience: Software developers, 25–40 years old
- Positioning: Clear, memorable, scalable, implementation-ready

## Visual Direction
- Geometry-first composition with strong silhouette recognition
- High-contrast palette with one dominant primary color
- Typographic tone: Contemporary sans-serif with confident rhythm

## Logo Constraints
- Must be vector-native and scalable
- Must work at small (16px favicon) and large sizes
- Avoid decorative clutter and fragile detail

## Evaluation Criteria
- Distinctiveness across 3 concept directions
- Legibility at app-icon scale
- Consistent style for downstream poster generation
```

## Logo Guidelines to Document

Include these constraints in the brief when relevant:

- **Clear Space:** Maintain clear space around logo equal to height of primary symbol
- **Minimum Size:** Digital 120px width (full), 40px (icon); Print 1 inch / 0.25 inch
- **Don'ts:** Do not rotate, stretch, add shadows, or distort the logo

## Voice & Tone (Brand Personality)

When describing brand personality in the brief:

- **Professional** but not corporate
- **Technical** but approachable
- **Innovative** but reliable

**Writing style:** Use active voice; be concise and clear; use "we" and "you" rather than "I" or "one".
